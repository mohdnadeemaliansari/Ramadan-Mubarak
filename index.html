About Ramadan

Ramadan is the ninth and one of the most sacred months of the Islamic calendar. It is a month of fasting, prayer, self-discipline, and spiritual purification. Muslims around the world observe fasting from dawn (Sehri) until sunset (Iftar).

Ramadan is special because the Holy Quran was revealed during this blessed month. It is a time when Muslims increase their worship, recite the Quran, perform Taraweeh prayers, and seek forgiveness from Allah.

Fasting in Ramadan is one of the Five Pillars of Islam and teaches patience, gratitude, and compassion for the poor and needy. Muslims also give charity (Zakat and Sadaqah) to help those in need.

Scientific Benefits of Ramadan Fasting

Ramadan fasting is not only a spiritual worship, but modern science also proves that fasting has many health benefits for the human body. It helps in improving physical, mental, and emotional well-being.

Improves Metabolism

Fasting helps balance insulin levels and improves metabolism which supports better energy use.

Helps in Weight Loss

During fasting, the body burns stored fat for energy which supports healthy weight management.

Detoxifies the Body

Fasting gives rest to the digestive system and helps the body remove toxins naturally.

Improves Heart Health

Studies show fasting can reduce bad cholesterol (LDL) and improve heart health.

Conclusion: Ramadan fasting is a perfect combination of spiritual purification and scientific health benefits.
